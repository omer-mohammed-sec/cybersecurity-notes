\# Cybersecurity Security Roles Overview



This document provides an overview of key cybersecurity roles and their responsibilities.



---



\## Security Analyst

\- Protects organisational data and systems

\- Analyses networks and overall security posture

\- Works with stakeholders across the organisation

\- Writes security reports and recommendations

\- Develops security plans based on threats and trends



---



\## SOC Analyst (Level 1)

\- Monitors security alerts and events

\- Investigates suspicious activity

\- Uses SIEM tools and log analysis

\- Escalates incidents when required



---



\## Security Engineer

\- Designs and maintains security controls

\- Implements security solutions

\- Mitigates vulnerabilities and risks

\- Defends against web and network-based attacks



---



\## Incident Responder

\- Responds to active cyber attacks in real time

\- Works under high-pressure environments

\- Uses MTTD, MTTA, and MTTR metrics

\- Performs post-incident analysis and reporting



---



\## Digital Forensics Examiner

\- Investigates digital crimes and incidents

\- Collects digital evidence legally

\- Maintains chain of custody

\- Documents and reports forensic findings



---



\## Malware Analyst

\- Analyses malicious software

\- Performs reverse engineering

\- Uses static and dynamic analysis techniques

\- Requires strong Assembly and C knowledge

\- Documents malware behaviour



---



\## Penetration Tester

\- Tests systems for vulnerabilities

\- Performs ethical hacking

\- Assesses risk and business impact

\- Reports findings with remediation advice



---



\## Red Teamer

\- Simulates real-world attackers

\- Tests detection and response capabilities

\- Maintains persistence while avoiding detection

\- Provides actionable attack insights
